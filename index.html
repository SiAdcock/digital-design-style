A guide to digital design at The Guardian

06/02/18

IntroductionThe Guardian website is made up of a series of fronts and articles.

IntroductionFronts are broken down into containers, these allow stories to be organised in a logical manner

IntroductionContainers are composed of modular blocks of story cards

IntroductionCards can hold an image, kicker, headline, standfirst and meta information

IntroductionEach card type is designed to give a sense of the tone of the story

IntroductionThrashers are custom designed containers usually promoting single articles or series (app thrashers are different)

Grids & SpacingThe grid unifies the way we arrange information on all our digital platforms

Grids & SpacingThe desktop grid is based on columns of 60px with 20px gutter

Grids & SpacingWe use 4 fixed grid sizes on large screens Wide — 1300px, LeftCol — 1140px Desktop — 980px, Tablet — 740px

Grids & SpacingAnd a fluid grid on mobile devices

Grids & SpacingSpacing is used intentionally to reference keep the design language consistent across print and digital

Grids & SpacingThe placement of the typography

Grids & SpacingThis alignment is consistent from headlines on cards and container titles to bylines in article pages

Brand UseThe Guardian has two expressions of its brand

Brand UseThe logo and the roundel are always either aligned top right or bottom right

Brand UseThe roundel acts as a simplified expression of the brand — often used to mark the Guardian’s own content on and off platform. For more detailed information please visit theguardian.com/brand-hub

TypographyThe Guardian has two main typefaces and three additional typefaces

TypographyGuardian headline is designed to allow for the optimal reading experience across all platforms and is used across the website and the app for headlines giving them a unique character and voice

TypographyGuardian Titlepeice is an accompaniment to The Guardian’s logotype, it’s bold and striking, use cautiously and appropriatley

TypographyOur display typefaces are matched by highly legible text variants

TypographyWe use our sans serif for small sizes or meta information

Colour PaletteWe have a colour palette that allows us to be bold, playful, tasteful and sensitive.

Colour PaletteOur colour palette is designed to aid readers in navigating therefore consistency is important

Colour PaletteColour is an important tool for both the UI and editorial, each pillar has it’s own colour palette, it contains a main colour, a feature variant and an inverted variant, we also have an extensive neutral palette

Graphic elementsWe use various devices to create hierarchy, structure and help people navigate

Graphic elementsWe have two styles of button. Consider weighting when choosing a style: the lozenge is bolder and the circular button is more subtle. Icons are used to help the user navigate and sit centrally inside an imaginary circle at the edge of the button

Graphic elementsWe use highlighting to create impact, help aid hierarchy and emphasis within designs

Graphic elementsRules give structure, they also help to add a subtle difference in flavour across articles. We have 4 different types that get used across the website.
