Cards & Containers

The Guardian website is made up of a series of fronts (?) and articles.

image: front with multiple containers wireframed

Fronts are broken down into containers which ultimately promote articles

image: single container wireframed

Containers are composed of modular blocks of cards

image: same container with content

Cards can hold an image, kicker, headline, standfirst and meta information

image: single card zoomed in

Thrashers are custom designed containers usually promoting single articles or series (app thrashers are different)

image: the minute — erica — close encounters

Grids & Spacing

Spacing is used intentionally to reference the Guardian’s brand and create enough space to breath

image: Something nice (maybe custom design)

The use of typography and other elements often references the placement of the guardian logo on the newspaper masthead

image: Newspaper cover

This alignment is consistent from headlines on cards and container titles to bylines in article pages

image: Headline on card container title and byline

The grid unifies the way we arrange information on all our digital platforms

image: Show grid

The desktop grid is based on columns of 60px with 20px gutter

image: Show grid with measurements

We use 4 fixed grid sizes on large screens

image: four breakpoints with measurements

And a fluid grid on mobile devices

image: iphone se and galaxy whatever shit phone that is

Brand Use

The Guardian has two expressions of its brand

image: The roundel and the logo

The logo and the roundel are always either aligned top right or bottom right

image: White box with logo aligned to the top

The roundel acts as a simplified expression of the brand — often used to mark the Guardian’s own content on and off platform

image: Thrasher and twitter feed

Typography

The Guardian has two main typefaces and three additional typefaces

image: Typeface waterfall

Guardian titlepiece is our brand typeface

image: Big titlepiece

For headlines and large sizes we use Display Egyptian and occasionally Display Sans

Our display typefaces are matched by highly legible text variants
